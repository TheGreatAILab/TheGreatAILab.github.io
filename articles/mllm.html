Towards Large Physical Models

Course Overview

From LLMs to Multimodal Foundation Models and Large Physical Models

This course introduces multimodal foundation models as a key stage in the evolution of modern AI. Large language models mainly take text as input and generate text as output. Multimodal foundation models extend this paradigm by connecting language with images, audio, video, and other sensory signals, enabling AI systems to understand, generate, and reason across multiple forms of information.

Looking forward, multimodal foundation models will further evolve into large physical models. Future AI systems will not only process text, images, audio, and video, but also understand physical geometry, object motion, spatial relations, actions, and real-world constraints. The long-term goal is to move from language intelligence to multimodal intelligence, and finally toward physical intelligence that can perceive, reason, predict, and act in the real world.

This course will introduce advanced and frontier techniques in multimodal foundation models. The content is not limited to any existing textbook. Instead, it will combine fundamental concepts with recent research progress, representative model architectures, emerging training paradigms, and future directions toward large physical models.
